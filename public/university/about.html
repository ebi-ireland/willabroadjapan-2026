The Problem We Solve

Cost is the primary barrier

Despite strong academic profiles and genuine motivation, most Japanese students who aspire to study abroad face a single, decisive obstacle: cost. Tuition and living expenses at international universities — particularly in the United States — often exceed ¥6,000,000 per year. Will Abroad was founded to remove this barrier.

How We Work

Partnership, not brokerage

We do not charge students finder's fees. Instead, we negotiate scholarship packages directly with partner universities — awards issued under each institution's own name. This model aligns the incentives of students, families, and universities around one shared goal: a successful enrollment.

Selection Philosophy

Broad eligibility. Rigorous screening.

We believe talent is widely distributed but opportunity is not. Our eligibility criteria are deliberately inclusive — any Japanese-fluent individual without a bachelor's degree may apply, including gap-year students, community college students, homeschoolers, and working adults. From this broad pool, we select through a two-stage process: a document review followed by an online interview. Only candidates who genuinely match a partner institution's culture and expectations are recommended for admission.

Partnership Process

How a partnership
works in practice

01

Initial Conversation

We schedule a call with your International Office or Admissions team to understand your enrollment goals, scholarship capacity, and preferred student profile.

02

Scholarship Framework Agreement

Together we agree on a scholarship package — the award level, eligibility conditions, and the number of students — which your institution formalizes under its own name.

03

Candidate Referral

Will Abroad opens applications, conducts document review and interviews, and delivers a shortlist of pre-screened candidates whose profile matches your institution's expectations.

04

Enrollment & Ongoing Support

We provide pre-arrival orientation and maintain contact with enrolled students throughout their studies, reducing attrition and supporting a positive experience for both student and institution.
